Course Policies

Grading and Participation

If you are enrolled in my course, you need to ask at least four non-trivial questions during the lectures and the four questions need to be in separate lectures. It is up to you to decide whether the question merits non-trivial status. In the final, you will be asked to self-report if you completed this participation requirement.

Participation is a 1/0 multiplier in terms of your grade. Meaning that if you report that you did not ask at least four questions, the course grade is an automatic 0.0 but I commend you for your honesty. Otherwise, your grade is defined by 60% homework, 20% midterm, and 20% final.

Late Policies on Homework

When I was an undergraduate, most of my math courses had a deadline of Friday for homework. At that time, problem sets were printed or hand-written and dropped off in a mailbox in the math department. But it was well known that the grading graduate student didn't check the mailbox until they returned to campus on Monday morning.

In that spirit, homeworks in this course will be due on Gradescope on Fridays at 5 PM. But homework will only be considered late if it is not turned in by the time our graders start grading on Monday. Anything past that timestamp will be graded with a 50% demerit if it is turned in before the final begins.

To be extra clear: when our graders start grading is a random variable and not in your control. I will not tolerate any complaining on this matter. If you want to guarantee your homework is graded accurately, you should turn it in on Friday.

Any extenuating circumstance should be discussed with the instructor by Thursday.
